My Favorite Musical Artist: Owl City

(Image Credit: Universal Republic)

My favorite musical artist is Owl City, a.k.a Adam Young. You might know Owl City from its popular singles, "Fireflies", "Vanilla Twilight", and "Good Time". A common misconception is that Owl City is a band or Adam Young’s stage name, when in fact Owl City is an electronica musical project created by Adam Young. His other musical projects include Port Blue, which focuses more on imaginative instrumentals, and Sky Sailing, which has a more acoustic sound.

The first Owl City songs I listened to were "Fireflies" and "Vanilla Twilight". These two songs stuck with me because of their unique sound and poetic lyrics. "Fireflies" is whimsical and evokes a sense of childlike wonder, while "Vanilla Twilight" is a moving dedication to losing someone and accepting that loss.

What I like most about Owl City is its ability to create an atmosphere within its songs, combining imaginative lyrics with electronic instrumentals. I believe the logic of Owl City's lyrics, especially in its earlier music, is not as important as how its songs make you feel. For example, in "Fireflies", he sings:

"'Cause I'd get a thousand hugs
From ten thousand lightning bugs
As they tried to teach me how to dance."

When I hear these lines, I picture a humorous scene, where fireflies are surrounding a child, who is attempting (and failing) to copy their movements. These lines also make me nostalgic for my childhood, when life was simpler, and my imagination ran wild. In "Vanilla Twilight", he sings:

"But drenched in vanilla twilight
I'll sit on the front porch all night
Waist-deep in thought because when I think of you,
I don't feel so alone."

When I hear these lines, I picture the singer sitting on his front porch, staring wistfully at a twinkling night sky. I feel the sadness associated with the singer’s loss, but also the hope that, with time, the singer will come to accept it and live life happily once again.

His songs also cover a variety of tones and topics. For instance, "If My Heart Was a House" is a romantic ballad, while "Fireflies" relates to his struggle with insomnia. Also, "Humbug" is a humorous, upbeat holiday song, whereas "This Isn't The End" is a poignant song about a daughter coming to terms with her father's suicide. "Verge" is a cheerful graduation tune, while "The Real World" ponders whether reality is worth living in. My favorite Owl City song is “House Wren”, which is a lighthearted song about a house wren’s journey to find a home.

Owl City released its first EP, Of June, in 2007, and 13 years later, it is still producing music. In fact, its sixth album, titled Cinematic, was released in June of last year. This album differs from its previous albums in that it is autobiographical, covering topics such as Young’s birth, how he met his current girlfriend, and his very first record deal.

Owl City is an artist with a unique sound, and I highly recommend its music!
